Southern African travel destination

Zimbabwe, a summary.

This beauty is a landlocked country of Southern Africa and it shares borders with 5 others: Botswana, Namibia, Zambia, Mozambique and the Republic of South Africa. The capital and largest city is Harare, followed by Bulawayo. Before it was named Zimbabwe (from the phrase "dzimba dzemabwe" meaning houses of stone and also after a The Great Zimbabwe Ruins), it was known as Rhodesia up until it gained it's independence from Britsh rule on the 18th of April 1980.

Weather

Zimbabwe only experiences 2 seasons: hot & rainy summers from September to May then cold and dry winters in the remaining 3 months. This is perfect for those who love to chase the sun. Summer temperatures range from 14 to above 30 degrees celcius and in winter they range from 5 to 20 degrees celcius depending on the location.

Culture

The country is made up of people from different cultures and beliefs, with 16 official languages. The 2 main tribes are the Shona people who make up the largest part of the population, followed by the Ndebeles and 13 other interesting tribes that are spread all over the country. Zimbabweans are known for being very friendly, welcoming and loving as it is part of their culture and traditions.
